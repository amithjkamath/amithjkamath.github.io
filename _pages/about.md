My name is Amith and I work on interesting problems related to Images and AI. I am data driven, and I like math as applied to gaining a better understanding of what we see. Most >= 2 dimensional representations of data tickle my neurons, and I find it amazing that face-swapping is a consumer feature, and automated radiology scans these days are as or more accurate than humans (caveats excluded).

Since 2010, image/vision related applications have fascinated me. My graduate dissertation focused on reducing MRI acquisition times and enabled accurate orientation measurement of white matter fibers in our brains. I've then dabbled in software development, and most recently building curriculum, running workshops/seminars/webinars for academia.
